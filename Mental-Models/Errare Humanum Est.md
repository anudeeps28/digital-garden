# Errare Humanum Est

## Idea
To err is human — making mistakes, wandering, and iterating is how we are biologically wired to learn.

## Definition
"Errare humanum est" — to err is human. Our brains are designed to learn through trial and error, not through perfect execution. We evolved as curious, wandering creatures; sitting still and following rigid rules is a cultural imposition, not our nature. The tragedy is that modern education and work systematically shame mistakes, creating anxious adults who are afraid to try. The antidote: take things less seriously, gamify everything, and see iteration as the method, not the failure.

## Source
Latin proverb; personal reflection on education systems and human nature.
