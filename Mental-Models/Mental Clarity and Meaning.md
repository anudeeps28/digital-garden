# Mental Clarity and Meaning

## Idea
Clarity and meaning are the antidotes to anxiety, frustration, and sadness — and they must be actively cultivated.

## Definition
Mental clarity is knowing what matters and why. Meaning is the felt sense that your actions connect to something real. Together, they form a stable foundation for wellbeing. Without them, the mind fills with anxiety, frustration, anger, and aimlessness. Many of the other notes in this garden — Stoic principles, productivity frameworks, philosophy of work — are ultimately strategies for achieving this state. Key inputs: reducing noise ([[News is Weaponized]]), building restorative practices ([[Calm and Peace]]), and connecting work to intrinsic values ([[You Cannot Compete with Someone Who Is Having Fun]]).

## Source
Personal synthesis across Stoic philosophy, positive psychology, and personal experience.
