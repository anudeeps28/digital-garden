# About Time (2013 Film)

## Summary
A British romantic comedy about a man who can travel back in time. The emotional arc isn't about changing the past — it's about learning to be fully present in the ordinary moments of life. The film's resolution: Tim stops traveling back in time and simply tries to live each day as if he's deliberately returned to it.

## Key Ideas
- Life is not about fixing mistakes but about being fully present in the unremarkable moments
- Time is the one resource that cannot be recovered — use it on what matters
- "Try and marry someone kind" — the wisdom of simplicity in what we value

## Quotes / Excerpts
> "I just try to live every day as if I've deliberately come back to this one day to enjoy it as if it was the full final day of my extraordinary, ordinary life."

> "We're all travelling through time together every day of our lives. All we can do is do our best to relish this remarkable ride."

> "The best we can do is do our best."

## My Thoughts
The film reframes time travel as a metaphor for presence. We already have everything we need — the practice is noticing it.
