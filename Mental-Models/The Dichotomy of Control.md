# The Dichotomy of Control

## Idea
Distinguish between what is in your control and what is not — then focus only on the former.

## Definition
The central principle of Stoicism: some things are up to us (our thoughts, judgments, desires, actions) and some things are not (other people's opinions, external events, outcomes). Suffering comes from trying to control what we cannot. Freedom comes from releasing what falls outside our sphere and pouring energy into what we can actually influence.

## Source
Stoic philosophy, particularly Epictetus (*Enchiridion*).
