# Stoicism

## Idea
Stoicism is a philosophy of focusing on what you can control and accepting what you cannot, in order to live virtuously and peacefully.

## Definition
Stoicism is an ancient Greek and Roman philosophy (Zeno, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius, Seneca) built around a few core insights: (1) The world is largely outside our control; (2) Our responses, judgments, and values are within our control; (3) Virtue — wisdom, courage, justice, temperance — is the only true good; (4) External things (wealth, health, reputation) are "preferred indifferents" — nice to have, but not necessary for a good life. Practiced consistently, Stoicism produces resilience, equanimity, and clarity.

## Source
Epictetus (*Enchiridion*), Marcus Aurelius (*Meditations*), Seneca (*Letters*).
