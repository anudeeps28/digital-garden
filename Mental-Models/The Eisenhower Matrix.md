# The Eisenhower Matrix

## Idea
Sort all tasks into four quadrants by urgency and importance to maximize meaningful output and eliminate noise.

## Definition
A prioritization framework that divides tasks into four quadrants:
1. **Urgent + Important → Do Now** (crises, deadlines; minimize time here)
2. **Important, not Urgent → Schedule** (deep work, long-term goals; *live here*)
3. **Urgent, not Important → Delegate** (interruptions, some meetings)
4. **Neither → Delete** (busywork, distractions; ruthlessly cut)

The key insight: most people live in Q1 and Q3 while neglecting Q2 — where all meaningful, strategic work happens. Dwight Eisenhower used delegation aggressively to protect his time for Q2 thinking.

## Source
Attributed to Dwight D. Eisenhower; popularized in productivity literature.
