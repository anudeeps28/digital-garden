# Digital Garden

## Idea
A digital garden is a personal knowledge base built on connections rather than chronology — a living document of evolving thought.

## Definition
Unlike a blog (posts ordered by date, polished and published) or a notebook (private, unstructured), a digital garden is a public or semi-public collection of notes at various stages of development — Seeds (new ideas), Budding (developing ideas), and Evergreens (mature, lasting insights). Notes connect to each other via [[bidirectional links]], creating a web of ideas that mirrors how the brain actually works. There are no folders in the brain, only thoughts connected to other thoughts. The garden metaphor captures the gradual, iterative nature of knowledge cultivation.

## Source
Ness Labs (nesslabs.com); Roam Research community; personal practice.
