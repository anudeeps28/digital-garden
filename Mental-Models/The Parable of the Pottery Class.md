# The Parable of the Pottery Class

## Idea
Quantity of practice produces better quality than focused pursuit of perfection — doing beats planning.

## Definition
A teacher split a pottery class in two: Group A would spend 30 days studying technique and produce one perfect pot. Group B would make one pot per day for 30 days — 30 pots total. At the end, every one of the best pots came from Group B. By making 30 pots, they accumulated feedback, iterated on failures, and developed muscle memory. Group A, having spent all their time in theory, produced inferior work despite their deliberate focus on quality.

The lesson: action is the path to mastery. You cannot think your way to excellence; you have to do your way there.

## Source
David Bayles & Ted Orland, *Art & Fear* (widely retold parable).
