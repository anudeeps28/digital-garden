# Hedonic Adaptation

## Idea
Humans quickly return to a baseline level of happiness regardless of positive or negative life changes.

## Definition
Hedonic Adaptation (also called the Hedonic Treadmill) is the psychological tendency to rapidly normalize to a new baseline of happiness after major life events — both positive and negative. Win the lottery or lose a car; within weeks, subjective wellbeing returns to its prior level. This trait evolved as a survival mechanism: the ability to adapt to extreme circumstances (temperatures, food scarcity, loss) is what allowed humans to persist and thrive.

The implication: acquiring things to achieve lasting happiness is a losing game. Wellbeing is a practice, not a possession.

## Source
Psychology research; personal observation.
