# Consistency Over Intensity

## Idea
Moderate effort applied daily produces better long-term results than sporadic bursts of maximum effort.

## Definition
Going all-out all the time leads to burnout, injury, and eventual abandonment. A better model: choose an effort level you can sustain indefinitely — something like 70-85% — and apply it daily. The compounding of small, regular actions over weeks and months outperforms occasional heroic efforts followed by recovery periods. This applies to exercise, creative work, learning, business-building, and any other domain where time-in-practice matters.

## Source
Personal observation; [[85% Rule]] from Carl Lewis; James Clear on habits.
