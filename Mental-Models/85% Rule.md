# 85% Rule

## Idea
Performing at ~85% effort consistently outperforms going all-out, because sustainable pacing beats burnout.

## Definition
Popularized by Olympic sprinter Carl Lewis, who often trailed at 40m but led at 100m. His secret: while competitors gave 100% and exhausted themselves early, Lewis gave 85% — maintaining consistent form through the finish line and passing everyone in the final stretch. The lesson extends to all long-term endeavors: working out 20 minutes daily for a year beats 2-hour sessions every two weeks. The body and mind cannot sustain maximum effort indefinitely; moderate, consistent effort compounds.

## Source
Carl Lewis's training philosophy; James Clear's writings on consistency.
