# Pareto Principle

## Idea
80% of outcomes come from 20% of inputs — identify the vital few, ignore the trivial many.

## Definition
Also known as the 80/20 Rule, the Pareto Principle states that roughly 80% of consequences come from 20% of causes. In practice: 80% of results come from 20% of effort. This doesn't mean you can get away with 20% work forever — the remaining 20% of results requires a disproportionate 80% effort to achieve. The principle is most useful for identifying the minimum effective dose: the small set of skills, tasks, or behaviors that produce most of the value.

## Source
Vilfredo Pareto's observation about land ownership in Italy; widely applied in business and productivity.
