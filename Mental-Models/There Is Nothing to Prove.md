# There Is Nothing to Prove

## Idea
There is no race, no audience, and no legacy — once you internalize this, you become free to do what actually matters to you.

## Definition
The drive to prove yourself to others — to haters, doubters, family — comes from a wounded ego, not genuine ambition. And even if you win that race, you discover there was no race. Everything is temporary: money, status, property, legacy. The Earth itself will be uninhabitable in billions of years. Given the cosmic scale of impermanence, the only rational response is to do what you genuinely want to do, bring your ego as low as it can go, and have fun. This is a thought to return to when feeling overwhelmed, anxious, or competitive.

## Source
Personal reflection; related to Stoic and existentialist philosophy.
