# Acceptance

## Idea
Accepting reality as it is — rather than as you wish it were — is the foundation of equanimity.

## Definition
Acceptance is not passive resignation; it is the active recognition that some things cannot be changed and that fighting them costs more than it gains. It is the psychological move that [[The Serenity Prayer]] and [[The Dichotomy of Control]] both point toward: distinguish what is in your power, act there, and release everything else. Without acceptance, uncontrollable events generate endless frustration. With it, energy is freed for what actually matters.

## Source
Stoic philosophy; ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy); personal reflection.
