# Mere Urgency Effect

## Idea
We habitually choose urgent tasks over important ones, even when the important tasks offer greater rewards.

## Definition
The Mere Urgency Effect is the psychological tendency to prioritize tasks with short completion windows (urgent) over tasks with larger long-term outcomes (important), simply because urgency feels more compelling in the moment. Important tasks are harder, more ambiguous, and further from goal completion — so our brains default to the certain, immediate payoff of checking off something with a deadline.

## Source
Academic research (Journal of Consumer Research, 2018); personal observation.
