# Show Your Work

## Summary
Austin Kleon's manifesto for sharing creative work in progress rather than waiting until it's perfect. The central argument: in the age of the internet, being findable and generous with your process is as important as the work itself. Sharing your work-in-progress attracts collaborators, builds an audience, and makes creativity sustainable.

## Key Ideas
- You don't have to be a genius to share — share your process, not just the finished product
- "Become a documentarian of what you do" — capture and share the journey
- Amateurs are often better teachers than experts because they remember what it's like not to know
- Be a connector: share what you love, give credit, and build community

## Quotes / Excerpts
> "You don't have to be a genius. You just have to be yourself."

> "Share something small every day."

## My Thoughts
The core insight — that sharing work-in-progress is itself a creative act — resolves the perfectionism trap. You don't wait until you're good enough. You get good by sharing.
